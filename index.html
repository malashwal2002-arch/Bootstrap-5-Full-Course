Frequently Asked Questions

The peak season is from September to late March. During these months, the nights are long and dark enough to provide the perfect backdrop for the Aurora Borealis. We recommend booking your tour during a "New Moon" phase for the darkest skies and best visibility.

Warm layers are essential. Since we spend several hours outdoors in Arctic conditions, we recommend thermal base layers, a wool mid-layer, and a windproof/waterproof outer jacket. Don't forget insulated boots, a warm hat, and high-quality gloves!

Nature is unpredictable, but we've got you covered. While we have an 85% success rate, if the Aurora doesn't appear due to weather or solar activity, we offer a 50% discount on your next chase or a free seat on the following night's tour (subject to availability).
